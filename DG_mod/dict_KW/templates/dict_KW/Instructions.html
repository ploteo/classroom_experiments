Part 2

You will see again the 11 choices you just saw in Part 1.

For each of the choices, you will be asked to judge the social appropriateness of each Option A, relative to Option B. You can choose among 4 levels of social appropriateness:

  • Very appropriate
  • Somewhat appropriate
  • Somewhat inappropriate
  • Very inappropriate

The following is an example of the choice you are going to make. Choices are made by selecting an option from the dropdown menu.

(The choice on this page has no consequences on your actual earnings.)

On the next page, you will make a choice for each of the 11 allocations you faced in Part 1. Choices are made in a table, and each row contains a choice among the four social appropriateness levels.

At the end of the session, one of the 11 choices will be randomly selected for payment.

The payment is computed as follows:

  1. choices of all participants in the session are counted, and the social appropriateness level chosen by the majority is computed;
  2. if your choice is the same as that chosen by the majority, you earn an additional 5 tokens; if your choice is not the same as that chosen by the majority, you earn 0 tokens.
